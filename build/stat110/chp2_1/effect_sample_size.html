Examining the Effect of Sample Size¶

Example 2.3: Gender Discrimination, Revisited

Once again, consider the Gender discrimination example. Recall that of the 20 persons selected for management so far, only 9 (or 9/20 = 45%) were female. In an unbiased selection process, we expected to see a woman selected 60% of the time. We found that the probability of observing 9 or fewer women selected by chance if the company was in fact using a fair selection process was .1275.

Question:

  1. The three studies provide equally convincing statistical evidence that the selection process discriminates against women.
